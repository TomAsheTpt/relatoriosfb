# Favela Bras Grant Planning

## Summary

The meeting focused on preparing an institutional grant application and planning performances and workshops for Favela Bras in Germany and Brazil. Responsibilities were assigned: Tom will prepare and translate the application and project text for review, the Goethe‑Institut account and submission must be made from Germany, and the application requires an official invitation and a confirmed participant count to meet funding criteria. The group agreed that a workshop counts as one required event and a final concert or festival performance will serve as another.

Participants evaluated event options, debating the Rakotsky Festival, local street or market performances, and the Sunday parade; they concluded the parade is not feasible because Jugendmusikkorps members are committed to the parade. Logistics and unresolved items include an instrument list from Tom (bass drum, snare harness, cymbal), visa verifications, and the possibility of Roman contacting Dorothee Segera with application questions. Planning for a large street band settled on a full brass and sax section with substantial percussion—suggested counts include four trumpets, four trombones, five saxes and a tuba—with percussion numbers flexible. Accommodation is available locally in a holiday unit and via park management; rehearsals are planned Monday–Thursday with a Friday festival show, and uniforms will be yellow t‑shirts bearing an emblem once the GPG funding paperwork is provided. Funding documents will be sent to hosts so Roman can open an account to receive funds.
